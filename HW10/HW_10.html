Douglas Hill

Prior to the introduction of iron and steel, Native Americans made their tools and weapons from lithic(stone), bone, and wood. Quality materials to knapp projectiles and tools were not easily obtained. Not just any rock will do. North Carolina is home to one of the largest areas of high quality rhyolite/ rhyodacite/ and dacite resources on the east coast. The question that these maps and overlays help answer: 1.) Can we predict where the prehistoric quarry locations are located based on the underlying geological information? 2.) Can we track the materials obtained from a specific quarry to artifacts found at distant locations? 3.) Is the topography of the state coupled with the geology and indicator of higher quality materials that Native Americans utilized?
