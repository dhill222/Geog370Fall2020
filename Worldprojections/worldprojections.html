In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

I was able to change the projections by clicking on the lower right corner of the QGIS page and open a filter selector. In it I was able to search a large database of different projections. Using the filter search bar and some idea of what I wanted to project, I was able to select and apply the projection to my map. Each map was slightly different in the stretch and pull of the map.

Pseudo Mercator

The Pseudo Mercator is from a cylinderical view of the planet. The polar regions of the planet are stretched to relect that view.

Winkel Tripel

Accounts for some of the curvature but also blends in the mercator style maps

World From Space

INteresting perspective of the globe and areas are closest to even of the maps I examined.

World Mercator

Compressed map and stretched. South Pole seems to have an axtremely large land mass in relation to the rest of the planet.

World Sinusoidal ESRI 54008

I thought this was interesting because I can think of no good reason for this type of map projection. Very odd.

World Cylindrical Equal Area

Land massses at the equator seem to me much larger than areas north or south of it.

World Equidistant Conical

Top down view as if a cone was flattened. The earth seems normal close to the central axis and distorted as you move away from it.

North Pole Azimuthal

Very similar to the World Equidistant Conical projection. Difficult to determine any differences at first glance
